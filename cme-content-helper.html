Is the planning committee interprofessional?
Three things must all be true — not just one or two:

1. Planning committee: two or more professions involved in designing the activity — e.g. both a physician and a nurse, pharmacist, or social worker sit on the planning group.

2. Faculty presenters: two or more professions involved in delivering the content on the day — e.g. both a physician and a nurse present or facilitate sessions.

3. Team-focused design: the activity is designed to improve how the healthcare team works together, not just to update individual clinicians.

Planning collaboration alone is not enough. The interprofessional involvement must extend to who delivers the content as well.
The AI cannot infer this from the content — it determines eligibility for interprofessional education recognition.
