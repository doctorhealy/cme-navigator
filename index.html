CME Application Tools

Two tools to help educational planners navigate ACCME requirements, identify every credit opportunity, and produce compliant application content — designed for Michigan Medicine OCME&LL.

Available Tools
🗺️
Step 1 — Plan your application
CME Application Navigator

Answer 9–11 questions about your activity and get a complete picture of which application type to use, which credit types you qualify for, commercial support requirements, and what to flag to OCME&LL.

Produces
  • Application type determination (Live, Hybrid, RSS, Enduring)
  • MOC board credit eligibility — PARS & self-report boards
  • ACCME Commendation criteria matched to your activity
  • Michigan mandatory topic designations
  • Planner checklist & OCME staff build brief (PDF)
Open Navigator →
✦
Step 2 — Draft application content
CME Content Helper

Describe your topic, audience, and format. The AI drafts all seven ACCME-required narrative sections — practice gap, educational need, learning objectives, and more — ready to paste directly into MiCME.

Generates
  • Practice gap statement — 3 versions to choose from
  • Educational need diagnosis with root-cause reasoning
  • Expected results & educational format justification
  • Learning objectives aligned to Bloom's taxonomy
  • Competency mapping & full eligibility review
Open Content Helper →
Recommended workflow
1
Run the Navigator first
The Navigator identifies your application type, credit types, and commendation criteria — all information you need before writing application content.
2
Draft content with the AI Helper
Describe your activity and the Content Helper writes all seven required narrative sections. Every field is editable — treat it as a starting draft, not a final document.
3
Submit to OCME&LL
Print or save PDF supporting documents from either tool to share with your OCME&LL representative when you submit your MiCME application.
ℹ️

These tools support application preparation. All AI-generated content should be reviewed and edited to accurately reflect your planned activity. OCME&LL staff review all applications on submission and will guide you through any questions.
